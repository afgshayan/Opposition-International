OI Condolences — Former PM Begum Khaleda Zia

Opposition International mourns the passing of Begum Khaleda Zia, former Prime Minister of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, whose life was marked by unwavering dedication to public service and democracy.

Dec 30, 2025 Owen Paul Lippert 4 min read

Opposition International extends its deepest condolences to the family, supporters, and all who admired the life and legacy of Begum Khaleda Zia, former Prime Minister of the People's Republic of Bangladesh.

Begum Khaleda Zia's life was defined by her extraordinary commitment to public service and her courageous role in Bangladesh's ongoing struggles to solidify democratic governance. As a two-time Prime Minister and leader of the Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP), she stood as one of the most prominent opposition figures in South Asian politics.

A Life of Public Service

Khaleda Zia entered politics following the assassination of her husband, President Ziaur Rahman, in 1981. She rose to lead the BNP and served as Prime Minister from 1991 to 1996, and again from 2001 to 2006. Throughout her career, she championed democratic principles, contested authoritarian tendencies, and gave voice to millions of Bangladeshis who sought accountable governance.

Her tenure in office saw significant economic reforms and efforts to strengthen democratic institutions. Even when out of power, she remained a forceful advocate for political pluralism and the rights of the opposition to participate fully in the democratic process.

Political Challenges and Endurance

The later years of her political career were marked by immense personal and political challenges. She endured prolonged illness, imprisonment on charges that many international observers questioned, and political turmoil that tested Bangladesh's democratic foundations. Through it all, Begum Zia remained a symbol of resilience for those who believed in the necessity of a strong, independent opposition.

A Legacy for Democracy

Her story underscores a truth that Opposition International holds central to its mission: democracy requires not only those in government but those willing to stand in opposition — to hold power accountable, to offer alternative visions, and to endure personal sacrifice for the principle that citizens deserve choices.

Paying Respect

Opposition International joins mourners in Bangladesh and around the world in paying respect to Begum Khaleda Zia. Her life serves as a reminder that the struggle for democracy is ongoing, and that those who dedicate their lives to public service — especially under the most difficult circumstances — deserve our recognition and gratitude.

May her legacy inspire future generations of democratic leaders and opposition voices in Bangladesh and beyond.
