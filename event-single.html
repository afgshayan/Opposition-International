Role of Opposition in Resisting Democratic Erosion

A virtual event on democratic resilience — exploring how opposition movements counter authoritarian trends and safeguard democratic norms worldwide.

About This Event

Around the world, democratic institutions face unprecedented challenges. From executive overreach to the erosion of civil liberties, opposition parties and movements are often the last line of defense for democratic governance. This virtual event brings together leading voices in democratic resistance to explore strategies for strengthening opposition in the face of authoritarian trends.

Join Opposition International for an in-depth discussion on how opposition movements can build resilience, forge coalitions, and leverage international solidarity to protect democratic norms and institutions.

What You'll Learn

  • Opposition strategies for countering democratic backsliding
  • Coalition building across political, ethnic, and regional divides
  • Media engagement and public communications for opposition movements
  • International advocacy and leveraging global solidarity networks
  • Legal frameworks for protecting opposition rights and political freedoms

Who Should Attend

This event is designed for activists, policymakers, researchers, and advocates engaged in democratic resistance and governance reform. Whether you work with an opposition party, a civil society organization, or an international body focused on democracy, this event will provide practical insights and networking opportunities.

Speakers

Led by Owen Lippert, Director of Opposition International, and guest speakers from regional chapters across Asia, Africa, and Latin America. Our panelists bring first-hand experience in democratic opposition from diverse political contexts.
